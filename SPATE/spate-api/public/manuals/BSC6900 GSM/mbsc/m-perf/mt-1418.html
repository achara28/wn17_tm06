Description

The BSC uses the handover algorithm to determine whether a handover is required based on the measurement reports reported by the MS. If the originating cell and the target cell are controlled by the same BSC, an internal inter-cell handover is initiated by the BSC. This specific counter provides the number of incoming internal inter-cell handover requests in the target cell in corresponding conditions. With the Incoming Internal Inter-Cell Handover Responses and the Failed Incoming Internal Inter-Cell Handovers, these counters determine the incoming internal inter-cell handover performance. With the counters related to the outgoing internal inter-cell handover, these counters determine the internal inter-cell handover performance.

The following counters are measured based on the target channel types and the frequency bands between which the handover is performed.
  • H3200W:CELL.INTRABSC.INCELL.HO.REQ.SD.900.900
  • H3200X:CELL.INTRABSC.INCELL.HO.REQ.SD.1800.1800
  • H3200Y:CELL.INTRABSC.INCELL.HO.REQ.SD.900.1800
  • H3200Z:CELL.INTRABSC.INCELL.HO.REQ.SD.1800.900
  • H3209W:CELL.INTRABSC.INCELL.HO.REQ.TCH.900.900
  • H3209X:CELL.INTRABSC.INCELL.HO.REQ.TCH.1800.1800
  • H3209Y:CELL.INTRABSC.INCELL.HO.REQ.TCH.900.1800
  • H3209Z:CELL.INTRABSC.INCELL.HO.REQ.TCH.1800.900
The following counters are measured based on the loop types. The loop types include the BSC local loop and BTS local loop.
  • H320a:CELL.INTRABSC.INCELL.HO.REQ.BSC.LOOP
  • H320b:CELL.INTRABSC.INCELL.HO.REQ.BTS.LOOP

Measurement point

As shown in Figure 1, the specific counter is incremented by one at A when the BSC decides that an internal inter-cell handover is required based on the measurement report. The measurement object is the target cell.
