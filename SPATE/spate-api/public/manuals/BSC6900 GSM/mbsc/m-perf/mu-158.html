GSM Cell to GSM Cell Incoming Handover Measurement(CALL.IntoCellHo.GCELL_GCELL)

Overview

The GSM cell to GSM cell incoming handover involves all incoming inter-cell handovers with the target cell as measurement object, including the handover initiated by the BSC after receiving a measurement report from an MS by considering the channel interference, cell load, and the signal quality and signal level in the measurement report, and the handover initiated by the BSC after receiving an ASS REQ from the MSC (the assignment procedure can be normal assignment procedure, directed retry procedure, or mode modification procedure). This measurement is involved as long as the handover is coming from one cell to another cell.

After receiving the handover indication (intra BSC), HO REQ (inter BSC), or the directed retry handover request from the assignment procedure determining module, the service procedure processing module of the BSC sends a HO REQ message to the target cell (inter BSC) or request the channel from the target cell (internal inter-cell) through the MSC. After the channel or resources request succeeds, the BSC sends a HO CMD to the MS. After the the MS hands over to the target cell through the HO ACC, PHY INFO, FIRST SABM frame, and UA frame messages, the MS sends a HO CMP message to the BSC and releases the resources of the originating cell. In this case, the handover is complete.

This measurement can be used for assessing the reasonability of the neighboring cell priorities and providing reference for removing neighboring cells. It is usually used in periodic network optimization.

The measurement object for GCELL_GCELL is cell pair. For example, if there are 10 neighboring cells for cell A, the number of measurement objects that use cell A as the source cell is 10. The statistics on measurement objects for GCELL_GCELL may increase the CPU load in the BSC. If you need to measure this type of counters, measurement must be initiated on the M2000.
