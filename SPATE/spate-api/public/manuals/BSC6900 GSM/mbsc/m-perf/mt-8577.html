Description

When decoding the customized extended MR or pre-processed customized extended MR from the BTS, the BSC checks whether the VQI cell is contained in the MS. If the cell is contained, the BSC decodes current VQI.

VQI is the MOS score of the voice quality.

For WBAMR call, when the BTS reports the VQI value, the VQI value is sent to the BSC after being multiplied by 50 and converted into an integer because it is a floating point value. After the BSC receives the VQI value, the VQI value ranges from 0 to 5.1 after being divided by 50. The measurement is performed with VQI 0.5 as a level. The number of VQI 0-0.5 level measurement reports indicates the number of measurement reports with the voice quality smaller than 0.5. Other levels are measured in the same way.

A specified counter is incremented by one based on the VQI.

VQI establishes the mapping between radio performance and voice quality and uses the MOS analysis method to provide the rankings of voice quality. MOS is used to assess the quality of the middle and low-rate voice coding. The MOS value ranges from 1 to 5.
  • 5: Excellent
  • 4: Good
  • 3: Fair
  • 2: Poor
  • 1: Unsatisfactory

Measurement point

After receiving a customized extended MR or pre-processed customized extended MR from the BTS, the BSC decodes the MR. The specified counter is measured based on the VQI (N <= WBAMR VQI < M) when the VQI cell is contained in the MS and the BSC decodes the value of the VQI. M and N refer to the level described above. M ranges from 0 to 5, N ranges from 0.5 to 5.1.
