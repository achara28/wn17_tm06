Description

This measurement provides the average erasion rate of each channel type. The channel type is categorized in to FR, HR, EFR, AMR FR (12.2 kbit/s), AMR FR (10.2 kbit/s), AMR FR (7.95 kbit/s), AMR FR (7.40 kbit/s), AMR FR (6.70 kbit/s), AMR FR (5.90 kbit/s), AMR FR (5.15 kbit/s), AMR FR (4.75 kbit/s), AMR HR (7.95 kbit/s), AMR HR (7.40 kbit/s), AMR HR (6.70 kbit/s), AMR HR (5.90 kbit/s), AMR HR 5.15 kbit/s, AMR HR (4.75 kbit/s).

Measurement point

The BTS reports the channel type, number of erased frames, and total number of frames in a granularity period in the uplink measurement report. After receiving the measurement report, the BSC measures the frame erasion rate based on the formula as follows: Number of erased frames * 10000/total number of frames in a granularity period. The purpose of multiplying the erased frame ratio by 10000 is to obtain more precise digits after the decimal point. The value is accumulated to Accumulated FER, and MRs with FER are incremented by one. The Average FERs of various channel types are measured on the basis of the formula as follows: Accumulated FER/(MRs with FER * 100).
