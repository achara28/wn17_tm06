Description

These counters provide the rank distribution of the uplink voice quality of FR services carried on a TRX in a measurement period.

The VQI provides a method for direct measurement of the voice quality for radio network and establishes a corresponding relational model for the radio network performance and voice quality. Based on the radio quality parameters of the uplink and downlink voices, the VQI scores are calculated to determine and qualify the voice quality within the network, thus providing a basis for network optimization. The VQI employs the mean operating score (MOS) method of analysis to rate voice quality. MOS analysis is a subjective method for rating medium- and low-rate voice coding quality. MOS values ranges from 1 to 5.

  • 5: Excellent
  • 4: Good
  • 3: Fair
  • 2: Poor
  • 1: Unsatisfactory
The VQI ranks the voice quality into 11 levels, with a score difference of 0.5 between adjacent levels. During a call, the number of times that the voice quality is in each level is recorded. Then, together with "Average Measurement Value of the FR UL VQI (Voice Quality Index) in the Customized MR", these counters indicate the uplink voice quality of FR services carried on a TRX in a measurement period.

Measurement point

After receiving a customized extended MR or pre-processed customized extended MR from the BTS over the FR channel, the BSC decodes the MR. The specified counter is measured based on the VQI (N <= FR VQI < M) when the VQI IE is contained in the MR and the speech version is FR.M and N refer to the level described above. N ranges from 0 to 5, and M ranges from 0.5 to 5.1.
