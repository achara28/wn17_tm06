Measurement related to Algorithm per NodeB(ALGO.NODEB)

Overview

The NodeB algorithm measurement involves the Iub congestion measurement, NodeB unavailability measurement, and dynamic spectrum sharing measurement.

Iub congestion measurement is aimed to check whether the configured Iub bandwidth can meet the requirements of UEs under the NodeB. Based on this measurement result, operators can perform fault location or determine whether capacity expansion is required and. The RNC periodically monitors the bandwidth usage of each Iub path under the NodeB. If the remaining bandwidth is less than a preset threshold, the RNC determines that congestion occurs and sends a congestion indication message. After the RNC detects that the remaining bandwidth is more than the preset threshold again, it sends a congestion elimination message.

NodeB unavailability measurement is aimed to detect the condition that a NodeB becomes unavailable due to a fault or misoperation. This measurement can be used to analyze the impact of the fault or misoperation on services.

Dynamic spectrum sharing enables the UMTS network to use some spectrums of the GSM network when the GSM traffic is light. Dynamic spectrum sharing measurement is used to assess the performance of this function.
